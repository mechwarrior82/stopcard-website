Modern Technology

Our Modern society demands speed and ease of access. The creation of enhanced wireless technologies has given us a high level of convenience, but everyone should be cognizant of a fully digitized world. The latest in wireless technologies have enabled card manufacturers to store your personal information in a miniature microprocessor that is embedded within your credit/debit cards and I.D.s – this microprocessor uses wireless signals to communicate. This technology is known as RFID - Radio Frequency Identification. It allows a remote reading device to transmit a wireless data collection signal to your RFID enabled cards in order for your cards to transmit back your stored personal information. Passports now include these RFID chips that contain very important and unique information about you. Many government I.D.s and personal bank cards have already incorporated this RFID technology.

The Risk

All it takes to access this critical personal information is a simple, readily available, low cost RFID reader. RFID enabled chip cards can be scanned and read right through your purse, wallet or pocket. The reader can be hidden out of sight and can be used without you ever being aware that it is scanning you. This wireless technology gives identity thieves the ability to steal your personal information without ever having direct contact with your credit cards or other personal belongings. Electronic Pickpocketing is a threat that is difficult to recognize, but you must protect yourself.

The Impact On You

Identity Theft is one of the worst things that can happen to you. It takes significant time, money and effort to recover from such an attack against you. You are often unaware of such an attack until you are ready to make an important transaction such as buy a new home or car.

Frequently Asked Questions

How Does RFID Work?
When your RFID enabled credit/debit card is placed in the vicinity of a RFID reader, the reader emits a specific frequency that makes the RFID enabled smart card broadcast all of your personal information in order to complete the requested transaction.
How does the STOPCARD® work?
The STOPCARD® creates an electronic shield around your RFID enabled credit/debit cards and I.D.s. This shield prevents your RFID enabled cards from responding to an RFID reading device.
What type of cards does it work on?
Any RFID device that uses the 13.56 MHz frequency. This includes US passports and all RFID credit and Debit Cards. It can even protect your security ID badge used for contactless secure entry systems.
What is its protection range?
Protects typical credit card distribution within your wallet. So a single STOPCARD® properly placed in proximity of the cards in your wallet can protect all cards in the wallet.
Is it legal to use the STOPCARD®?
The STOPCARD® complies with US FCC requirements for Passive RFID devices. It is protected by U.S. Patents 7889056, 8102243, 8237549, 8305193.

Other U.S. and International Patents are pending.

A basic foil sleeve only has a Passive defense system.

The STOPCARD® allows you to see all your credit cards for ease of access when needed.
